Q.1
IDENTIFY DISTRIBUTION
CLO 1
Identify the most suitable distribution (Binomial / Poisson / Normal) for each random variable X:
  • The event of a loan application being approved or rejected out of 200 applicants
  • The event of the number of website visits per minute
  • The weight of packaged products in a factory
  • The blood pressure levels among adults in a population
  • The event of the number of earthquakes occurring in a region per year
  • The event of patients responding positively or negatively to a treatment
Q.1
SOLUTION
CLO 1
  • Loan approved/rejected → 2 outcomes, fixed n = 200 BINOMIAL
  • Website visits per minute → count of events per interval POISSON
  • Weight of products → continuous, bell-shaped measurement NORMAL
  • Blood pressure → continuous, symmetric distribution NORMAL
  • Earthquakes per year → count of rare events per region/time POISSON
  • Patients respond positively/negatively → 2 outcomes, fixed n BINOMIAL

Q.10
NORMAL · FIND μ AND σ²
CLO 2
Monthly earnings follow Normal distribution. Given: 33% earn more than RM 824 and $P(X<800)=0.0228$.

Estimate the mean and variance (nearest integer).
Q.10
SOLUTION
CLO 2
Eq.(1): $P(X>824)=0.33$ → Z-table: $z=0.44$$\dfrac{824-\mu}{\sigma}=0.44 \;\Rightarrow\; \mu+0.44\sigma=824\cdots(1)$
Eq.(2): $P(X<800)=0.0228$ → left tail: $z=-2.00$$\dfrac{800-\mu}{\sigma}=-2.00 \;\Rightarrow\; \mu-2\sigma=800\cdots(2)$
Solve simultaneously: $2.44\sigma=24 \Rightarrow \sigma\approx10$, then $\mu\approx820$
ANSWER
$\mu=820$  ·  $\sigma^2=100$
